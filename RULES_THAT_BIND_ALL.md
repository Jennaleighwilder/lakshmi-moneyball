# RULES THAT BIND ALL 12 MARKETS
## Common Threads Across the Entire Betting/Gambling Landscape

**Purpose:** Find what constrains every market—the shared rules, regulators, and restrictions.  
**Compiled:** 2026-03-02

---

## 1. FEDERAL REGULATORS (The Big Binders)

| Regulator | What It Binds |
|-----------|---------------|
| **CFTC** | Prediction markets, event contracts, derivatives, commodities, futures. Exclusive jurisdiction over Kalshi, Polymarket, CME prediction markets. |
| **SEC** | Stocks, options on stocks, securities. Binds Category 5 (stocks/options). |
| **FinCEN** | AML (Anti-Money Laundering). Binds casinos, sportsbooks, platforms handling money. |
| **State Gaming Commissions** | Sports betting, casinos, lottery, DFS. Varies by state. |

**Key:** If it's a "bet or wager" under federal law, it's either exempt (CFTC/SEC regulated) or illegal. UIGEA exempts: CFTC-registered, SEC-registered, certain fantasy sports.

---

## 2. FEDERAL STATUTES (The Law That Binds)

| Law | What It Does |
|-----|--------------|
| **UIGEA (2006)** | Prohibits accepting payments for *unlawful* internet gambling. Defines "bet or wager" and exempts CFTC/SEC-regulated activity. |
| **Wire Act** | Prohibits wire transmission for sports betting across state lines. |
| **Bank Secrecy Act** | AML programs required. Casinos must file CTRs, SARs. |
| **Commodity Exchange Act** | CFTC authority. Event contracts = commodity derivatives. |

**Common thread:** Legality depends on *where* you are and *what* you're doing. CFTC-regulated = federal preemption. State-licensed = state rules.

---

## 3. AGE (Universal Binder)

- **18 or 21** depending on state and product.
- Lottery/horse: often 18.
- Casino/sportsbook: often 21.
- **No federal minimum**—state sets it.
- **Utah & Hawaii:** Effectively no legal gambling (18/21 irrelevant).

---

## 4. GEOGRAPHY (State Binds)

| Rule | Applies To |
|------|------------|
| **Utah** | No gambling. Period. |
| **Hawaii** | No gambling. Period. |
| **New York** | No Crypto.com prediction markets; restricted DFS at times. |
| **Other states** | Each has its own matrix: sports yes/no, DFS yes/no, casino yes/no. |

**Common thread:** You must be in a state where the product is legal. Platform geo-blocks enforce this.

---

## 5. KYC / AML (Identity Binds)

- **KYC:** Verify identity before play. ID, address, sometimes SSN.
- **AML:** Monitor deposits, withdrawals, suspicious activity.
- **Source of funds:** Platforms can ask. Large deposits = more scrutiny.
- **Prohibited:** Minors, self-excluded, sanctioned persons, certain jurisdictions.

**Common thread:** Every regulated platform does this. No KYC = offshore/unregulated.

---

## 6. INSIDER TRADING / MANIPULATION (Conduct Binds)

| Rule | What's Prohibited |
|------|-------------------|
| **Material non-public information** | Trading on info you're not supposed to have. |
| **Controlling the outcome** | Betting on something you can influence. |
| **Misappropriation** | Using confidential info in breach of duty. |

**CFTC (Feb 2026):** Anti-fraud and anti-manipulation apply to event contracts. Insider trading = enforcement risk.

**Example:** Kalshi suspended MrBeast employee who traded on video outcome with production info. 200+ insider investigations in a year.

**Common thread:** If you *know* or *control* the outcome, you can't bet. Same idea across prediction markets, sports (game-fixing), options (tipping).

---

## 7. SELF-EXCLUSION (Voluntary Binder)

- **What:** You opt out. Platform must block you.
- **Scope:** Single operator or multi-operator (e.g., state-wide).
- **Duration:** 6 months, 1 year, 5 years, or lifetime.
- **US:** 1-800-522-4700 (national helpline). State programs vary.

**Common thread:** Once you self-exclude, you're bound until the period ends.

---

## 8. DEPOSIT / LOSS LIMITS (Responsible Gaming Binds)

- **Deposit limits:** Cap on how much you can add (daily/weekly/monthly).
- **Loss limits:** Cap on how much you can lose.
- **2025:** Many jurisdictions require time-based and loss-based protections.
- **NJ example:** Alerts at $10K/24hr or $100K/90 days.

**Common thread:** Platforms offer tools; some jurisdictions mandate them.

---

## 9. WINNING TOO MUCH (The Sharp Binder)

- **Sportsbooks:** Can limit or ban winning bettors. "Recreational" vs "sharp."
- **Not illegal** for the book to do this—it's their house.
- **Result:** Sharps move to offshore, exchanges, or get limited.
- **Prediction markets:** Less common (peer-to-peer, no house).

**Common thread:** If the *house* loses to you, they can stop doing business with you. Exchanges/prediction markets: different—you're vs other users.

---

## 10. TERMS OF SERVICE (Contract Binds)

Every platform has ToS. Common clauses:
- **Arbitration:** Disputes go to arbitration, not court.
- **Account closure:** Platform can close your account.
- **Prohibited conduct:** No bots (sometimes), no VPN, no collusion.
- **Settlement disputes:** Platform decides; you agree to their process.

**Common thread:** You agree to their rules when you sign up. Violation = ban, forfeit.

---

## 11. RESOLUTION / SETTLEMENT (Outcome Binds)

| Market | Who Decides |
|--------|-------------|
| **Prediction** | Source agency (Kalshi), UMA oracle (Polymarket). |
| **Sports** | Official league/result. |
| **Options** | Exchange, expiration rules. |
| **Casino** | RNG, game rules. |

**Common thread:** You don't get to argue the outcome. The designated authority decides. Disputes go through platform process.

---

## 12. MONEY IN / MONEY OUT (The Cash Binder)

- **Deposit methods:** Bank, card, crypto (where allowed). Some methods restricted.
- **Withdrawal:** KYC complete, sometimes cooling-off, processing time.
- **Taxes:** Winnings are taxable. 1099s for certain thresholds.
- **Reversal:** Chargebacks, fraud = account frozen.

**Common thread:** Moving money in/out is monitored and regulated.
